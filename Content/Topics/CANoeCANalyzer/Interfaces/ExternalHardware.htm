GL Logger Suite

You can use some loggers as network interface devices in your CANoe DE product. The logger is connected to the CANoe DE product computer via Ethernet connection. Once the measurement begins, the logger sends the data to the CANoe DE product. The data can then be analyzed in the Measurement Setup. You cannot send data.

GPIB

GPIB is a protocol for networking external instruments. These instruments include measuring devices, power supplies and function generators. This standard was originally developed by Hewlett Packard under the name HP-IB. Later HP-IB was renamed GPIB (General Purpose Interface Bus) by the IEEE Committee as. Other equivalent names are IEEE 488 and IEC 625..

IOpiggy

The IOpiggy is a further piggy-back module for the VN8900 and VN7570, complementing the CANpiggy and LINpiggy. This piggy makes measuring and generating I/O signals simple and easy.

PortLink

PortLink is used to link CANoe DE product environment variables to analog and digital I/O ports on I/O cards. The value of an environment variable is either read to a port or output by a port.

RS232

In your CANoe DE product you can control the RS232 interface via CAPL functions. If your computer does not have a built-in RS232 interface, you can use a USB to RS232 adapter. Be sure to install the corresponding adapter driver when doing so. You can also use other network interfaces with the RS232 API. The requirement for this is that Windows must recognize these serial network interfaces as serial COM ports. One example would be IrDA, as most are represented as a virtual COM port in Windows.

Scope

Option Scope is an integrated oscilloscope solution for your CANoe DE product, based on a USB scope hardware. The PicoScope 5444B-034 hardware supports up to 2 CAN, 4 LIN or 2 FlexRay channels. The scope is triggered using the sync line of Vector's interface hardware. With bus-specific trigger conditions the cause of protocol errors can be located much faster than with a traditional oscilloscope.

VN 1600 Family

The VN1600 product line succeeds the market-proven USB CANcaseXL interface. While the VN1610/VN1611/VN7610 with 2 channels focuses on minimum size and weight, the VN1630/VN1640 with 4 channels is designed for flexibility and I/O support.
The VN8900 system is a systematically advanced development of the proven products of the XL-Interface family and can be used for a wide variety of applications. A particular focus here is on parallel access to multiple bus channels and I/Os with exacting realtime and latency requirements.

VN7570

The VN7570/VN7572 is a high performance PCI Express based network interface. It offers up to eight channels of which four may be assembled with CAN, LIN and up to one (VN7570) or two (VN7572) FlexRay transceivers. This makes the VN7570/VN7572 highly flexible and applicable for a wide variety of applications.

VN8900

The VN8900 combines the ease of use (plug & play, USB â as e.g. VN1630) with real-time capability. For that purpose in CANoe the real-time-relevant parts of the simulation- and test environment are executed on the VN8900 device (see Realtime Execution (RT Setup)). In CANalyzer the send branch and its blocks are executed on the VN8900 device. Thereby you achieve improvements with respect to latency times and precision of timers.

VIO System

VIO System is a compact solution that enables the test of electronic components such as sensors, actuators, or even complete controllers at a very early stage of development (board level test). For VIO System, various input and output cards for a wide range of applications are available, including analog and digital inputs and outputs, current measurement, and more.

VT System

The VT System is a modular hardware system that connects an ECU's I/O lines for testing purposes.
